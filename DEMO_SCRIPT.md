## Tips

- **The flip is money shot #1.** Don't rush it. Let the card sit on the front for 3 full seconds. Let the audience read the gullible reader's "I'd think" text. Then flip. Silence during the animation is more powerful than narration.
- **The agent is money shot #2.** When the tool calls appear ("Searching document..." â†’ "Reading clause analysis..."), let them breathe. The judges need to SEE that Opus is using tools autonomously, not just generating text. The dollar answer with the breakdown is the payoff.
- **The Expert Mind loading screen is your Opus 4.6 showcase.** It's the only moment in the video where judges SEE the model thinking. Don't skip it â€” but don't linger more than 4 seconds.
- **Use the lease**, not insurance. Everyone has signed a lease. Late fee cascades are viscerally relatable. "$12,300 in total exposure" makes people gasp.
- **Show a green card.** A tool that flags everything loses credibility. One green card ("Confirmed: fair as written") proves calibration.
- **The "I don't write code" line** is your differentiator. You're a journalist who built 14,000 lines through conversation with Claude. That story IS the hackathon story.
- **Pre-run the analysis** before recording. Start recording AFTER the lease is already analyzed. Show the upload screen first for context, then cut to the loaded analysis. Record the Expert Mind loading screen in a separate take and splice it in.
- **Pre-type the question.** Have "What happens if I'm 3 months late on rent?" ready to paste. Don't waste video time on typing.
- **One take is fine.** Loom records screen + voice together. The judges want authenticity, not production value.
- **Don't demo**: countdown timer, tricks panel, dark mode toggle, keyboard shortcuts, session history. They fill wait time in live use but slow down a scripted video.
