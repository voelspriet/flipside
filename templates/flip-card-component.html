Section 4.2

Late Payment Fee

"Tenant shall pay a late fee of $50 if rent is not received within five (5) days of the due date. This fee is in addition to any other remedies available to Landlord."
You'd think
This seems fair — five days is a reasonable grace period, and $50 isn't unreasonable for a late fee. Most leases have something like this.

Late Payment Fee

RED 72/100 Time Trap
The drafter's thinking
"The 5-day grace period generates approximately $40K in annual late-fee revenue across our portfolio. We set the fee just below the threshold where tenants actually push back or seek legal advice. The 'other remedies' language stacks this on top of our eviction rights."

What you should read The fee is a revenue strategy, not a courtesy. "Other remedies" means they can charge you AND start eviction proceedings simultaneously.
